The sample size is mostly based on the effect size: 
    - The effect size is likely moderate to large. 
      You're not comparing two similar LLMs — you're comparing meaningless auto-generated names against LLM-renamed ones. 
      That gap is substantial, meaning even a few hundred samples could be sufficient.
    - This means we only need 500 - 1000 samples

    - Power is simply the probability that your test will detect a real difference when one actually exists.
        - More samples → your metric estimates are more stable → smaller random fluctuations → easier to tell apart a real difference from noise.
    - Effect size answers the question: how big is the difference, in standardised units?
        - Large effect = the difference is so obvious it basically jumps out at you, even from a small sample.
